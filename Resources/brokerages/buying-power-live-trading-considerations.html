LEAN doesn't infer account type, leverage, margin requirements, or pattern day trading rules from your brokerage account. For example, if you have a cash account, and your algorithm doesn't set AccountType.CashCASH, LEAN will not automatically enforce cash account restrictions such as leverage of 1x or immediate settlement. You must manually configure your algorithm to adhere to these rules. If you set the leverage to 10x and you have a leverage of 2x, LEAN will place orders that can be rejected by your brokerage.

The default leverage of U.S. Equities is 2x. However, your brokerage can reduce the leverage of some securities based on market conditions. For example, leveraged ETFs such as TQQQ might have lower leverage during periods of high volatility. If your algorithm doesn't account for this, LEAN doesn't account for it and your orders can be rejected by your brokerage.
