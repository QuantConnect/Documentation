# Fundamental Property Paths in QuantConnect / LEAN

The Morningstar tree on `Fundamental` is large and deeply nested. `f.FinancialStatements.NetIncome` does not exist — net income lives on `IncomeStatement`, one level deeper. Use the lookup below instead of guessing from English names; a wrong path wastes a backtest run.


## How to read the lookup

- The first row, `Fundamental`, is the root — call this `f` below. It's the object passed into your `AddUniverse(...)` selection callback, and you can also pull a snapshot per-security: `var f = Securities["SPY"].Fundamentals`. Every other path chains from `f`.
- Each heading after the root is the accessor name from its parent. `CompanyReference` means `f.CompanyReference`; `IncomeStatement` is reached as `f.FinancialStatements.IncomeStatement`.
- Comma-separated values are properties on that node. Chain them: under `IncomeStatement`, `NetIncome` is `f.FinancialStatements.IncomeStatement.NetIncome`.
- A trailing `*` marks a `MultiPeriodField` wrapper — append a period accessor (most often `.Value`) to read the number. Unmarked properties return their type directly (number / string, or another node listed).
- The helper classes at the bottom (e.g. `MorningstarSectorCode`) hold named integer constants for comparison: `f.AssetClassification.MorningstarSectorCode == MorningstarSectorCode.Technology`. 

## Period accessors for `*` properties

- `.ThreeMonths`, `.SixMonths`, `.NineMonths`, `.TwelveMonths` — period-aggregated value (TTM at `.TwelveMonths`).
- `.Value` — most recent reported period (quarterly or annual, whichever filed last).
- `.OneMonth`, `.TwoMonths` — short-window aggregates (rare).

How to choose:

- **Income statement / cash flow**: prefer `.TwelveMonths` for cross-company comparability. `.Value` mixes quarterlies and annuals.
- **Balance sheet**: `.Value` — point-in-time snapshots; period aggregation is meaningless.
- **Ratio groups**: also `MultiPeriodField` — `.Value` unless you specifically want a longer window.

Forgetting an accessor is silent: the leaf compares as truthy and inequalities against numbers produce nonsense without raising.
