The DefaultBrokerageModel models buying power and margin calls to ensure your algorithm stays within the margin requirements. If you set the brokerage model to a different model, the new brokerage model defines how margin is modeled.

Buying Power

The DefaultBrokerageModel uses the SecurityMarginModel with a leverage of 1 for cash accounts. For margin accounts, the buying power model that's used and the amount of leverage that's available depends on the type of security that you're trading. The following table shows the buying power model that is used and the amount of leverage that's available for each security type when you are using a margin account:

Security Type

Equity SecurityMarginModel 2
Crypto CashBuyingPowerModel 1

Margin Calls

Regulation T margin rules are enforced with the DefaultMarginCallModel. When the amount of margin remaining in the portfolio drops below 5% of the total portfolio value, a warning is sent. When the amount of margin remaining in the portfolio drops to zero or goes negative, the portfolio sorts the generated margin call orders by their unrealized profit and executes each order synchronously until the portfolio is within the margin requirements.
