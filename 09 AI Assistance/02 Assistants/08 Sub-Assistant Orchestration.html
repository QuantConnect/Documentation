You can configure an assistant to involve other assistants in its work. Chained orchestration links assistants in a fixed sequence. The first assistant runs the task, its output is passed to the next assistant as input, and so on until the chain ends. Use a chain when the work breaks naturally into stages that each have a different specialist, for example an Ideas Assistant feeding a Research Assistant feeding a Backtest Assistant. For details on how the sequence executes at deployment time, see Chained Assistants.

Callable orchestration exposes other assistants as tools the assigned assistant can invoke when it needs them. This is similar to a regular tool call, except the "tool" is another assistant. Use callable orchestration when the path through the work is not known in advance. An assistant might call a Research Assistant in one deployment and a Live Monitoring Assistant in another, depending on what it finds. For details on how callable assistants behave at deployment time, see Callable Assistants.
